<h3>Build Quality</h3>
<p>The first thing to consider is build quality. Even in a budget device, durability is crucial. Look for a mouse that feels solid in your hand, with buttons that respond well to clicks. <h3>Sensor Type and Precision</h3>
<p>The type of sensor is vital for gameplay. Typically, budget gaming mice will feature either optical or laser sensors. Optical sensors tend to offer better accuracy for general gaming needs, while laser sensors can provide improved performance on various surfaces. Pay attention to the DPI (dots per inch), as it determines how sensitive the mouse is. Look for a mouse that has adjustable DPI settings, ideally ranging from 800 to 3200 DPI. This feature allows you to customize sensitivity based on your gaming preferences, providing a smoother targeting experience.</p>
