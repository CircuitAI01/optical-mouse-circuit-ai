<h3>The Early Mechanical Mouse</h3>
<p>The first computer mouse was invented in the 1960s by Douglas Engelbart. This early prototype was made of wood and featured two metal wheels that rolled along a surface. It translated physical movement into screen movement, allowing for a more intuitive way to navigate digital environments. The simplicity of this design was revolutionary at the time, paving the way for the mouse as an essential computing accessory.</p>
<h3>The Rise of Optical Technology</h3>
<p>By the late 1990s, the invention of optical mice significantly changed the landscape. These devices use a light-emitting diode (LED) and a light sensor to detect movement. This advancement provided greater precision and eliminated the need for a mouse pad, as optical mice could work on virtually any surface. As demand grew, manufacturers began designing more ergonomic shapes, catering to user comfort for extended computer sessions.</p>
