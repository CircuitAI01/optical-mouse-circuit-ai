<h3>Sensor Type and DPI</h3>
<p>The sensor type in a gaming mouse significantly affects its performance. The most common sensors are optical and laser. Here’s what to look for:</p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Optical Sensors:</strong> These are great for gaming as they offer better precision on most surfaces.</li>
    <li><strong>Laser Sensors:</strong> They can work on virtually any surface, but might lack the precision of optical sensors for fast-paced games.</li>
    <li><strong>DPI Range:</strong> DPI (dots per inch) measures sensitivity. A higher DPI means faster response, making it ideal for quick, competitive games. Look for mice that allow you to adjust DPI to fit your gameplay.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Polling Rate</h3>
<p>The polling rate indicates how often the mouse reports its position to your computer. A higher rate ensures smoother movement. Here’s a breakdown:</p>
<h4><a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=optical+mouse&tag=github-automation-20">Check Pricing in Amazon</a></h4><br><br><ul>
    <li><strong>Basic Rates:</strong> Most mice operate at 125Hz, meaning they report their position every 8 milliseconds.</li>
    <li><strong>Gaming Rates:</strong> Look for polling rates of 500Hz or 1000Hz for more responsive gameplay. This results in less input lag and a more fluid experience.</li>
</ul>
