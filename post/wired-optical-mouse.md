<h3>Understanding Wired Optical Mice</h3>
<p>A wired optical mouse uses light to detect its movement. Unlike traditional mice that rely on a ball, optical mice utilize a light-emitting diode (LED) paired with a sensor to track motion. The wired connection provides a consistent, lag-free experience, which is particularly beneficial for tasks requiring precision.</p>

<h3>Performance Insights</h3>
<p>The performance of a wired optical mouse can vary based on several factors, including build quality and technology. Here are some important aspects to consider:</p>
<h4>Sensitivity and DPI Settings</h4>
<p>The sensitivity of a wired optical mouse significantly impacts your experience. A mouse with a DPI range from 800 to 1600 is suitable for general tasks, while gamers may prefer devices offering DPI settings up to 4000 or more. Higher DPI allows for quicker responses, which can be crucial in fast-paced games.</p>
